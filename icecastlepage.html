Grimm began in 2020 as the moniker and experimental pop music project of Vancouver-based artist Sam Herle (formerly of the band Elf Pity), and is now a five piece band including Naia Mann, Cody Schroeter, Chris Bede, and Len Plisic. The project intertwines fantastical, genre-fiction inspired themes with everyday realism, and draws on an eclectic range of influences including electropop, early video game music, and indie rock.

"Your Head is a House", is Grimm's second album, and ventures away from solely indietronica to explore more of an indie rock sound as well, reflecting their genre-bending approach. The album was made and produced entirely by Sam Herle and Chris Bede in Bede's backyard studio, and for live shows they are joined by the full band. With their penchant for fantasy-inspired themes and their affinity for 80s retro aesthetics, the album encompasses a cosmic journey that combines elements of nostalgia, fantasy influences, and the allure of video game soundtracks. This captivating opus sets the stage for Grimm's future endeavors and their continued ascent in the music scene.
